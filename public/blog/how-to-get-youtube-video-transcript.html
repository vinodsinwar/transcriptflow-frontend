How to Get the Transcript of a YouTube Video (3 Easy Ways)

Updated July 2026 · 4 min read

Whether you're studying a lecture, quoting an interview, or repurposing a video into an article, you need the words as text. Here are the three practical ways to get a YouTube transcript, from the built-in option to the fastest one.

Method 1: YouTube's built-in transcript panel

YouTube itself can show a transcript for most videos:

  1. Open the video on youtube.com (desktop).
  2. Below the title, click the …more link in the description.
  3. Scroll down and click Show transcript.
  4. The transcript opens in a side panel with timestamps.

The catch: you can't download it, copying is painful (it copies line by line with timestamps mixed in), there's no export to SRT/VTT, and on mobile the option is buried or missing. It's fine for skimming — not for actually using the text.

Method 3: Browser extensions

Chrome extensions add a transcript button directly on the YouTube page. They're convenient if you need transcripts many times a day, but they require installation, permissions to read the pages you visit, and they only work in that one browser. For occasional use, a web tool does the same job with zero setup.

Which videos have transcripts at all?

All three methods rely on the video's caption tracks, so coverage is the same everywhere:

  • Manual captions — uploaded by the creator; the most accurate.
  • Auto-generated captions — created by YouTube's speech recognition for most spoken-word videos; quality is good for clear speech.
  • No captions — some videos (music-only, very new uploads, or captions disabled by the owner) have nothing to extract. Private videos can't be accessed either.

Frequently asked questions

Is it legal to get a transcript of a YouTube video?

Fetching captions of a public video for personal use — studying, notes, accessibility, quoting with attribution — is generally fine. If you plan to republish substantial parts of someone else's content, you need the rights to do so.

Can I get a transcript with timestamps?

Yes — both YouTube's panel and TranscriptFlow show timestamps. TranscriptFlow's timestamps are clickable links that open the video at that exact moment.

Can I get the transcript in a different language?

If the video offers translated captions, yes — see our guide to translating YouTube subtitles.
