How to Get a YouTube Transcript on Your Phone

Updated July 2026 · 3 min read

Quick answer: In the YouTube app, tap Share → Copy link. Open your browser, go to transcriptflow.io, paste the link, and tap Generate Transcript. The full transcript appears in seconds — no app to install, works on both Android and iPhone.

Why this is tricky on mobile

On desktop you can open YouTube's transcript panel, but in the mobile YouTube app that option is usually missing, and most browser extensions don't run on phones at all. Since roughly half of everyone searching for YouTube transcripts is on a phone, here's the reliable way that works on any device.

Step by step (Android and iPhone)

  1. Open the video in the YouTube app.
  2. Tap Share under the video, then Copy link.
  3. Open your browser — Chrome on Android, Safari or Chrome on iPhone.
  4. Go to transcriptflow.io and tap the paste field, then Paste the link (there's a Paste button on the field too).
  5. Tap Generate Transcript. The timestamped text appears in a few seconds.
  6. Tap Copy Text to copy it, or download it as TXT, PDF, or Word right on your phone.

Tips for phone use

  • From the browser instead of the app? Just copy the URL from the address bar — that works too.
  • Add it to your home screen. In your browser menu, choose "Add to Home Screen" so the tool opens like an app next time.
  • Reading a long transcript? The in-transcript search box lets you jump to any word without scrolling.
  • Another language? Use the translate option to turn the transcript into your language before copying.

No app, no signup, no storage. Everything happens in your mobile browser, and your video links and transcripts aren't saved.
